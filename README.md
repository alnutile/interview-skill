# Interview Quick Prep

A Claude Skill for the other side of the interview desk.

If you've ever dropped into a call and had to say "sorry, I haven't had a chance to look at your resume yet" — this is for you. It turns interview prep into a two-minute job.

## What it does

You paste the job description. You paste the candidate's resume. Claude hands you back a short brief:

- **Snapshot** — one or two sentences on who this person is
- **Strong matches** — where the resume clearly lines up with the role
- **Worth probing** — gaps and ambiguities to explore in the conversation
- **Questions to open with** — candidate-specific questions, not generic ones

No scores. No 10-page reports. A scannable brief you can actually read before the call starts.

## How to install

1. Download `SKILL.md` from this repo (or clone it)
2. Upload it to Claude as a skill, or drop it into your skills directory
3. Next time you have an interview, tell Claude "I have an interview coming up, help me prep" and paste the JD and the resume

## Who it's for

Hiring managers, recruiters, founders, or anyone who finds themselves about to interview a candidate without enough time to prep. It's for the interviewer's side specifically — not for candidates prepping to be interviewed.

## Why

Interviews are hard enough for the person showing up to them. Taking five minutes to know something about the candidate before the call is a small courtesy that makes a real difference.
